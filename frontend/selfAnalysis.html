Self-Discovery

Self Assessment

  • Step 1: Reflect on your emotions honestly.
  • Step 2: Identify recurring thought patterns.
  • Step 3: Note daily habits affecting your mood.

Learning

  • Step 1: Read about stress and resilience.
  • Step 2: Learn relaxation techniques.
  • Step 3: Apply one new strategy daily.

Goals & Planning

  • Step 1: Set realistic short-term goals.
  • Step 2: Prioritize and schedule them clearly.
  • Step 3: Review progress weekly.

Self Wellbeing

  • Step 1: Maintain healthy eating and sleep.
  • Step 2: Stay connected with loved ones.
  • Step 3: Practice gratitude and mindfulness.

What to Do During a Sudden Panic or Anxiety Attack

  • 1. Breathe slowly — inhale deeply, exhale calmly.
  • 2. Remind yourself it’s temporary and not life-threatening.
  • 3. Focus on one object around you to stay grounded.
  • 4. Use positive affirmations like “I am safe.”

How to Ensure No One in the Family Suffers from It

  • 1. Encourage open communication about emotions.
  • 2. Create a supportive and calm environment.
  • 3. Reduce stigma about mental health in family talks.
  • 4. Seek professional counseling when needed.

Problems Faced During Anxiety or Panic Attacks

People experiencing panic or anxiety attacks often face intense fear, sweating, shaking, and difficulty breathing. It can cause heart palpitations, dizziness, and feelings of detachment from reality. Emotional effects include isolation, fear of recurrence, and loss of confidence in social situations.
